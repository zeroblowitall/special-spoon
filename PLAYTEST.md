# The First Meeting — a playtest for two people

You are about to do something no game has asked of you before: grow two worlds apart on purpose, and then let them meet. It takes two people, about ten minutes a day, and three or four days of patience. The patience is the point.

## What you need

- Two people (call them **A** and **B**), each with any device that has a web browser
- Any way to send one file to each other — email, WhatsApp, USB stick, a shared folder

## Day one — two worlds are born

1. Both of you: download [`dist/driftgarden.html`](dist/driftgarden.html) and open it. A world is born the moment it opens — different land, different flora, different creatures, for each of you.
2. Spend five minutes: plant a few seeds, click your kith to meet them, **name one or two** (names make the later reunion land), and — important — **bless an emissary**. Your emissary is your token: when the worlds meet, it will lead.
3. Just close the browser when you're done. Nothing to save; the world keeps itself.

## Days two and three — drift

Visit once or twice a day, a few minutes each. Water something. Watch the weather. Read the Chronicle — things happen while you're away: friendships, storms, maybe a birth, maybe a tribe, maybe a passing. Open the **Lexicon** and watch your kith invent words — your world is developing a tongue of its own. If you're lucky, one of them will discover gardening; that's a red-letter day.

Don't coordinate with each other. The whole point is that your worlds become *different*.

## Day four — the meeting

1. Both of you: press **Preserve**. The game writes itself into a new file with your whole world inside.
2. Send your files to each other.
3. Each of you: open **your own** world, then **drag the other person's file anywhere onto the page**. (This is also the safety habit for life: *open files you made; merge files you receive.* Merging only reads a world's data and can never run anything.)

   📱 **On a phone?** Don't tap the received file directly — Android's built-in viewer can't run the game anyway. Open *your* world in Chrome, press *Merge worlds…*, and use the file picker.

Watch what happens. The land stays yours — but their plants settle into your soil, their kith walk out among yours, both histories braid into one chronicle, your emissaries meet at the meeting stone, **a child of both worlds is born**, and where your two languages named the same thing differently, the chronicle records the moment of first hearing.

4. Now the quiet miracle: compare notes. Your two merged worlds — one on each machine — hold **identical contents**: the same child with the same name, the same hybrid plant, the same braided history. Two files, two journeys, one family. (Different land and weather — the land is the one thing that stays your own.)

## What to tell us afterwards

- The moment that surprised you most
- Anything that confused you (be merciless — confusion is a bug)
- Your merge-child's name, and what your worlds called the rain
- Whether you'd hand a copy of your world to a third person

Open an issue on this repository, or just tell whoever gave you the file. Your world will keep going either way — it can't be shut down. That's the point.
