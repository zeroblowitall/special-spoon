# Day 6 — 8 July 2026

## Voices

The session the whole design has been building toward: **emergent language**. The kith now name their world — and no two worlds speak the same tongue.

## What got built (v0.6.0 — 109 KB, 87/87 checks green)

**The naming game** (Steels' classic, never shipped in anything like this): when a kith attends to something — a plant it just ate, the rain it's standing in, the sun, home, the water — it either uses a word it knows or **coins one in its own voice**, built from its genome's syllables. When two kith's paths cross, they chatter about whatever is nearby: agreement makes a word take root in both; disagreement ends with the surer voice carrying, and the coiner's name travels with the word forever. Within a world, vocabulary converges; between worlds, it diverges — automatically, with no world-level language state at all. Language lives *in the kith*, so it merges like everything else.

The determinism keystone, continued: a kith always coins the **same word for the same concept in every copy of the world** (seeded by its identity), so coinages reconcile across drifted copies like births and deaths do.

**Watched live in Hidden Clearing:** four kith gathered for twenty seconds and speech bubbles began — *"tapo"*, *"fefe"*. Their dictionary mid-convergence was textbook: the Mneivraex plant already unanimously **"tapo"**, rain settled on **"fefe"** with a rival *"irino"* still circulating, "home" a three-way contest. And a detail no one designed: most words were *first spoken by Irinonor* — the child born in yesterday's session is the talkative one naming the world. Emergent character.

**The Lexicon.** A new panel reads the world's tongue back: every named concept, its dominant word, surviving synonyms, and the first speaker remembered ("the rain — *fefe* — first spoken by Irinonor").

**Language contact on merge.** Because words live in kith, dialects meet the moment worlds do. On a first meeting the chronicle keeps the moment of first hearing — *"In A they call the rain 'fefe'; in B it is 'shava'. Both words live here now."* — written with a deterministic id and identical text on both sides (proven in the suite).

**Whisper-a-word** — the promised god-verb: once a day, from the Lexicon, you may whisper a word to your living emissary. It adopts your word with full conviction; whether it *spreads* is up to the naming game. Chronicled: *"Something on the wind whispered to Irinol, who now calls the Mneivraex 'omu'."* Verified end-to-end through the real UI.

**Tests grew to 87**, including: same-kith-same-word coinage determinism, two-speaker convergence with coiner attribution, population-level dominance, cross-world divergence, whisper validation/cooldown/chronicling, and dialect-contact entries identical across both sides of a merge.
