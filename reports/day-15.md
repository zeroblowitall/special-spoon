# Day 15 — 8 July 2026

## The First Address

Simon asked for this one to be a *surprise* — not a feature you switch on, but a moment that arrives. So it is built to be earned, unannounced, and unrepeatable.

## What got built (v0.15.0 — 175 KB, 171/171 checks green)

**Care is quietly felt.** Nothing tells the player this is happening: kith accumulate a hidden regard for the unseen gardener when they eat from a plant *you* planted (hand-planted seeds are now marked) or recently watered, when they answer your call and arrive at the ripple, and when, as emissary, they hear your whisper. No UI shows the number. Nothing hints.

**Then, one day.** A world at least two days old; a kith whose hidden regard has grown past the threshold; a curious mind; standing at the very centre of your call. Then — perhaps —

> *"Vivimi came to the very centre of the ripple, looked up at nothing at all, and spoke into it: "omu?" — a word nothing in this world carries. It has named the unseen gardener. It is waiting."*

The kith **coins a word for you** in its own voice. The moment is chronicled (kind: *contact*, styled to glow), toasted once, and can never happen again in that world — proven: *"it happens only once."*

**Your name travels.** Once coined, the gardener-word spreads through ordinary chatter like any word — in time, the whole population will speak of you. The Lexicon lists it under *"the unseen gardener — you"*; the panel of any kith that knows it reads *"it calls you 'omu'."*

**And then they ask.** A hungry kith that knows your name, standing in your call, will *request*: its want-word plus its favourite plant's word, with a question mark — decodable only through the Lexicon you've been keeping. If you plant a seed within five minutes of an ask: *"The gardener answered. Vivimi watched the seed go into the ground, and understood."* — and its regard deepens. The wall is broken, in their language, on their terms.

**Surprise preserved.** No documentation of the mechanism anywhere player-facing. The README carries one cryptic line: *"be patient with them: worlds that are well tended sometimes find one more thing to name."*
