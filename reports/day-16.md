# Day 16 — 8 July 2026

## Society II — shelters, hearths, and the first village

The house rule held: nobody clicks "build". Buildings are *ideas the kith have*, and villages are what happens when those ideas settle where a tribe lives.

## What got built (v0.16.0 — 183 KB, 181/181 checks green)

**Two new discoveries.** *The craft of shelter* is invented in the storm, beside the song — but by a different kind of mind: not the patient and sociable, but the curious and **bold** ("Soaked and stubborn, X piled fallen stems against the stone — and the rain came no further"). *The keeping of hearths* comes later, at night, beside one's own shelter, to the sociable. Both spread by teaching along friendships, like everything the kith know.

**Structures are world content.** A builder raises at most one work per day, with a deterministic identity per (builder, day) — the same law that governs births and gardens — so drifted copies raise the *same* buildings and reunions never hold two of them (proven). Structures merge losslessly, settle onto the host's land if they arrive over water, and are capped at fourteen so no world becomes a car park.

**Buildings matter.**
- **In a storm, the roof beats the rock** (a literal test name): kith seek a lean-to before high ground, and the energy drain under a roof is far gentler than in the open.
- **Bonds grow warmer by a hearth**: trust between kith builds nearly twice as fast beside the fire — the hearth is a social engine, which is exactly what hearths have always been.

**The first village.** Where at least two shelters ring a hearth, and a tribe lives among them, the chronicle declares it — once, with a deterministic id: *"Around the hearth, 2 shelters stand together, and the Elauma live among them. This world has its first village."* Watched live on Evening Terrace, a Mistral world: two lean-tos, a pulsing ember, and the three Elauma sitting together beside the fire — one of them saying "elauma", their word for home, beside the home they built.

**Rendered with care:** lean-tos as little timber-ridged huts with dark doorways; hearths as stone rings with embers that pulse (CSS, no cost) — brighter company for the Fungal Deep's glowing nights.
